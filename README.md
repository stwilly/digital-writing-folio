# digital-writing-folio
Sam's Digital Writing folio. Very important, very key.

**Who even *are* you, then?**

I'm a jack-all master of none, bouncing between scriptwriting, fiction prose, and "poetry" (but those last two really just exist on an odd spectrum, innit?). I have a low-orbit interest in interactive fiction—I've had Twine downloaded for years and maybe opened it once, but I'm willing to change! Please, I swear I will!

I've tried experimenting with not coming up with a structure for the whole of a piece before getting started but I'm just not good at it. Maybe one day. I rely too much on inspiration and things feeling easy enough. Tools-wise, most paper is just made to be trashed so I'll sometimes turn to it when the blinking cursor gets too much. I quite like sticky notes; good for jotting down an idea that I'll forget if I leave it for too long, they're probably my most-used tool, actually. 

Re: stickies, I find it useful to have ideas staring at me, I do the same with my uni assignments. Though generally my meatier ideas will graduate from being a sentence/word on a slice of paper, so the disparate stickies by themselves don't represent too much on their own. I use them for structuring as well, I find them less permanent than writing on a sheet of paper or typing it—they demand less, no need to worry about what comes first or last on a Post-It and you can only fit so much on them.

**- What are curious about writing in the digital environment?**

I usually enjoy weird stuff someway or another, e-lit feels very promising in that regard.
